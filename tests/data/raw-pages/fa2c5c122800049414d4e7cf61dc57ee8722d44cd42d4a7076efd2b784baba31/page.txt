== Proposed Article Reorganization ==
<!-- START PIN -->{{Pin message|}}<!-- [[User:DoNotArchiveUntil]] 19:50, 20 October 2033 (UTC) -->{{User:ClueBot III/DoNotArchiveUntil|2013450654}}<!-- END PIN -->
This article has a an unchecked status for structure for the B-class. There is good material here, but many results are not cited. Also, there is some replication of topics. The [[Solar sail]] article provides a better structure and is a good model for this related technology. Since most of magnetic sail is based upon analogy to the Earth's magnetosphere, theoretical, simulation, lab experiments and only a few proposed trials the outline at this time need not contain sections for actual flight experience. Therefore, I propose the following re-organized structure.

#Physical Principles
#Modes of operation
#Proposed magnetic sail systems
#Theoretical models
#Experimental results and trials
#Performance comparison
#Fictional uses in popular culture
#See also

Offline I have placed all of the existing material in this structure and have begun work on the uncited topics. I will continue working offline to collect this material, figures and citations so that parts of the above structure will not appear empty when I perform the reorganization.

Section heading added to outline. Anticipated using other citations, but Perakis & Hein 2016 citation used was a preprint and not WP:RS - so deleted that text. Also, exponent for magnetic sail force may have had an error in that and Sharma 2020 citation. Reached out to Perakis & Hein in August 2022, but so far have not gotten response.

Text from prior to article reorganization placed there cut and pasted below: for discussion:

The operation of magnetic sails using plasma wind is analogous to the operation of solar sails using the radiation pressure of photons emitted by the Sun. Although solar wind particles have rest mass and photons do not, sunlight has thousands of times more momentum than the solar wind. Therefore, a magnetic sail must deflect a proportionally larger area of the solar wind than a comparable solar sail to generate the same amount of thrust. However, it need not be as massive as a solar sail because the solar wind is deflected by a magnetic field instead of a large physical sail. Conventional materials for solar sails weigh around 7 g/m<sup>2</sup> (0.0014&nbsp;lb/sq ft), giving a thrust of 0.01 mPa (1.5×10<sup>−9</sup> psi) at 1 AU (150,000,000&nbsp;km; 93,000,000&nbsp;mi). This gives a mass/thrust ratio of at least 700&nbsp;kg/N, similar to a magnetic sail, neglecting other structural components.

The [[Solar sail|solar]] and magnetic sails have a thrust that falls off as the square of the distance from the Sun. [[User:Dmcdysan|Dmcdysan]] ([[User talk:Dmcdysan|talk]]) 03:30, 8 September 2022 (UTC)

== Some comments on the diagrams ==

1) They need an identifier to explicitly connect them to the main text that refers to them, and preferably a caption to explain what they show without having to find and read that main text.

2) The diagram of Relative Force versus Astronomical Units: This must be meant to illustrate the main text that says "the magnetic field falls off as 1/r^f0", so how can the red line for f0=1 stay at 100% regardless of distance? The red line should be where the blue line is, and the others below that.

3) The diagram immediately under the heading "Overview": Aren't these spiral paths what would happen for charged particles moving in a uniform "vertical" magnetic field, ie: constant and parallel to the black axis vector? The text that seems to refer to this is not nearby, and says "As shown in the illustration in the presence of a magnetic field shown in green, charged particles gyrate in circular arcs...". The "magnetic field shown in green" is circular rather than vertical, and the charged particles are moving in spirals rather than "circular arcs".

4) The main text following this goes on to discuss the gyroradius: "[At 1 AU], the distance from the Sun to the Earth, the gyroradius of a proton is ~72 km ...". Due to what magnetic field? The values 72 km and 36 nT (or 4x10^-8 nT) occur several times in the article, but it is not clear where the "36 nT" comes from. Is it the Earth's magnetic field, or the Sun's magnetic field, or what? A bit later, the gyroradius is ~500 km, but there is no mention why. Is this from the lower magnetic field of the interstellar medium, or from the spacecraft's own field generator? Since the ambient magnetic field is one of the conditions the magsail has to work within, it would help understanding to have this explicitly stated.
