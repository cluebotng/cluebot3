The Sinhalese people (Sinhala: සිංහල ජනතාව, romanized: Sinhala Janathāva), also known as the Sinhalese or Sinhala people, "Change x are an Indo-Aryan ethno-linguistic group Y" native to the island of Sri Lanka.[16][17] They are the largest ethnic group in Sri Lanka, constituting about 75% of the Sri Lankan population and number more than 15.2 million.[1][2]



Genetically, the Sinhalese are considered largely of Dravidian origin, while linguistically they speak an Indo-European (Indo-Aryan) language.

Explanation: 
The Sinhalese people speak an Indo-Aryan language. However, according to recent DNA studies published in 2025, the Sinhalese show significant genetic similarities with Tamil populations from Tamil Nadu, India, suggesting a substantial Dravidian genetic component in their ancestry. This finding contradicts earlier historical and genetic research, as well as Sinhalese chronicles, which proposed that the Sinhalese migrated from northern or northwestern India around 500 BCE.
