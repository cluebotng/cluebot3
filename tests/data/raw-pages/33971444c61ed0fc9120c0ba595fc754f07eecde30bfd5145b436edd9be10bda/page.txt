== Electronic component pitch ==

SMD components use the 0.1" pitch rarely. Isn't [[Surface-mount technology|SMD]] more popular now? <span style="color:#aad;display:inline-block;transform:rotate(-45deg)">❏</span>&nbsp;[[User:Okterakt|Okterakt]] <span style="color:#aad;display:inline-block;transform:rotate(45deg)">🦊</span> '''<span style="color:#5bcefa">U</span><span style="color:#f5a9b8">w</span><span style="color:#5bcefa">U</span>''' ([[User talk:Okterakt|talk]]) 15:28, 5 November 2025 (UTC)

:oops, I just reverted your change and then noticed this discussion. The vast majority of headers are based on 1/10-inch pitch. The vast majority of IC's with separate legs are also based on 1/10-inch or 1/20-inch. A very few use 0.2 mm (some ZigBee connectors). However, as you said, some SMDs (eg [[Ball grid array|BGA]] but not [[Quad flat package|quad packs]]) are based on 0.5 or 0.4 mm. Even in BGA packs, some use metric and some use 1/20-inch.<span style="border:1px solid blue;border-radius:4px;color:blue;box-shadow: 3px 3px 4px grey;">[[User:Stepho-wrs|'''&nbsp;Stepho&nbsp;''']]&nbsp;<span style="font-size:xx-small; vertical-align:top">[[User Talk:Stepho-wrs|talk]]&nbsp;</span></span> 02:16, 6 November 2025 (UTC)
